Generative AI — Design Thinking

Building a Real-World AI Assistant

CareerPath AI was developed as a practical exploration of generative AI technologies. Using Large Language Models (LLMs), Natural Language Processing (NLP), and prompt engineering, I built a conversational assistant that solves a real problem: helping students and early-career professionals navigate the job market.

The project followed the Design Thinking framework — Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test — ensuring the chatbot was built around actual user needs rather than assumptions.

  • Audience: Students, early-career professionals, hiring coaches, and AI practitioners
  • Purpose: Demonstrates applied AI development, prompt engineering, and human-centered design
  • Technologies Used: LLM, NLP, Conversational AI, Prompt Engineering
  • Framework: Design Thinking (5 phases)

Test Scenarios
Scenario 1
What are the requirements to become a data analyst?
The chatbot recommended learning Python, SQL, statistics, and data visualization tools such as Tableau or Power BI.
Scenario 2
How can I improve my resume?
The chatbot suggested highlighting achievements, using clear formatting, and tailoring the resume to match specific job descriptions.
Scenario 3
What interview questions should I prepare for a data analyst role?
The chatbot recommended preparing for SQL queries, statistical analysis, data visualization tools, problem-solving scenarios, and behavioral interview questions.

Professional Reflection

Workshop 1 — Reflection

Building CareerPath AI taught me that great AI tools are not just about the technology — they are about deeply understanding the people you are building for.

This project gave me hands-on experience with generative AI technologies and the full development lifecycle of a conversational AI assistant. From testing prompts with large language models to applying the Design Thinking framework, every phase deepened my understanding of how AI systems can solve real human problems.


One of the most valuable lessons was the power of prompt engineering. Early in the process, the chatbot's responses were either too general or too lengthy. By refining the prompts — making them more specific and context-rich — the quality of the responses improved dramatically. This showed me that how you communicate with an AI system is just as important as the system itself.


The Design Thinking framework kept the work grounded in empathy. Instead of building a tool based on assumptions, I started by identifying real challenges students face: lack of career direction, limited resume knowledge, and interview anxiety. That foundation shaped every decision in the design and testing phases.


Overall, this project demonstrated the practical potential of generative AI in education and career development — and gave me a concrete, shareable artifact that reflects both my technical curiosity and my commitment to building tools that genuinely help people.
